Wright State trustees take no action, strike to continue next week

Trustees met for nearly three hours in executive session today but convened the meeting without taking any vote. Around 100 or more students showed up to the 2 p.m. board meeting today to protest the lack of an agreement. Board chairman Doug Fecher emerged from the meeting and reiterated the board’s offer to negotiate a successor agreement that could take effect immediately once agreed to. Fecher again offered to clarify some of the terms of employment in order to reassure union members who might be concerned.
