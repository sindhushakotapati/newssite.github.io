The longest Government shutdown comes to a temporary end...

After a record-breaking 35-day budget impasse, President Donald Trump on Friday signed a bill that would temporarily open the federal government for three weeks.

Trump signed the measure after the Senate and House each passed it Friday, according to the White House.

Hundreds of thousands of federal employees are being asked to return to work and “reopen offices in a prompt and orderly manner” following the end of a 35-day government shutdown.The Office of Management and Budget sent a memo late Friday to the heads of shuttered departments and agencies after President Donald Trump signed a bill that temporarily reopens the federal government for three weeks.

With even some conservatives casting the agreement as a retreat by the president, Trump is tweeting that it “was in no way a concession” on his part.

The president told reporters Friday afternoon that he believes there is a good chance Democrats will agree to fund his border wall after he reached a short-term deal with congressional leaders to re-open the government. “We’ll work with the Democrats and negotiate and if we can’t do that, then ... obviously we’ll do the emergency because that’s what it is -- it’s a national emergency,” Trump said.
